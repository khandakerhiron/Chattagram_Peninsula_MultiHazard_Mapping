How It Works

Methodology

Both assessments follow the same MCDA-AHP pipeline — each factor layer is reclassified to a 1–5 risk scale, then combined using AHP-derived weights in a weighted overlay.

🏔

Landslide Susceptibility

13 conditioning factors covering terrain morphology, geology, vegetation, land use, proximity, and SAR-derived surface deformation.

  • Geology — USGS lithological units
  • SAR Deformation — Sentinel-1 Max & Mean
  • Terrain — Elevation, Slope, Aspect (SRTM)
  • Soil — BARC Map Portal
  • Rainfall — CHIRPS climatology
  • NDVI — Landsat 9
  • LULC — Dynamic World
  • Proximity — Rivers, Faults, Roads
🌊

Flood Risk Assessment

11 hydrological and terrain factors emphasising drainage conditions, water accumulation potential, and proximity to river networks.

  • TWI — Topographic Wetness Index (DEM)
  • SPI — Stream Power Index (DEM)
  • Curvature — DEM-derived
  • Terrain — Elevation & Slope (SRTM)
  • Soil — BARC Map Portal
  • Rainfall — CHIRPS climatology
  • NDVI — Landsat 9
  • LULC — Dynamic World
  • Proximity — Rivers, Roads
